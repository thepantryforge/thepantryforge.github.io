Okay, so basically, this website you're looking at right now is served by GitHub Pages over HTTPS, which is about as secure as your bank. The reason I haven't put much effort into making this site safe is because it has access to zero personal information. It's sectioned off from the rest of the business by a handful of Stripe's security protocols. All this site does is show you some products and tell your computer to remember which ones are in your cart. Then, when you enter the checkout, this website tells your computer to go to Stripe's (extremely secure) checkout page and tell it your cart. This website is like a sketchy salesperson in the middle of a store, there to tell you where everything is, but you wouldn't want to give them your credit card. Then you walk over to the checkout which is guarded by two burly security guards (Stripe). Then, once you place your order, the guards walk out to the back of the store and tell me what you ordered, but they don't give me your credit card.
So no, this site isn't very secure. But it's totally secure for what it's doing (showing you around the store). When you actually place an order, that site is probably more secure than the Pentagon.

Product Care

Simple! Don't mistreat it, don't leave it in direct sunlight, wash it with a scrub in cold water, and that's about it!

Online sources suggest a lifetime of 10-15 years is typical for well cared-for 3D-printed products. Please note that PLA is quite sensitive to sunlight. If you leave it in direct sun, it may only last 3-5 years.
